Chili con Carne

8 Servings

  • 2 tablespoons beef dripping or vegetable oil
  • 1400g minced beef, very lean
  • 4 small onions
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • 1 large bialetti of coffee, with 750ml water
  • 2 stock cubes (vegetable and beef)
  • 1 can of tomatos
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 4 squares dark chocolate
  • 3 dried ancho chillies
  • 3 small smoked jalapeno
  • 2 big dried cayenne peppers
  • 1 small habanjero
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds, toasted
  • 4 tablespoons oregano
  • 2 tablespoons mild chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons dark brown muscavado sugar
  • 4-5 coloured bell peppers
  • 3 cans of red kidney beans
  • 1 can pinto beans
  • 200g dried black beans (or 2 cans)
  • 1 can of pinto beans (or black beans)

Heat the fat in a large, heavy-bottomed pan on a high heat, then brown the mince in batches, stirring regularly: don't crowd the pan, or it will steam rather than brown. Add the onions and stir fry briefly, until slightly browned.

Turn the heat down and add the garlic, then the coffee, the stock, canned tomatoes, tomato paste and chocolate. Stir and cook until the onion has completey softened, covered, for 2 hours.

Meanwhile, cut the stalks off the dried chillies, take out the seeds and chop them finely, together with the roasted cumin seeds. Stir in the oregano and chilli powder.

If using dried black beans, cook them with 5-6 times the amount of water for about 70 minutes. Slice the bell peppers.

Add the spices to the pan along with the sugar. Then add the peppers and simmer, partially covered, for another half hour, adding a little water if it seems dry, or you prefer a saucier chilli. 10 minutes before the end of cooking, add the beans, taste, and adjust the seasoning and spicing if necessary.

Serve with:

Jacket Potato

Guacomole

Polenta
