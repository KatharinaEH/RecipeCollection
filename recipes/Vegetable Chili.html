Vegetable Chili

6 Servings

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 (red) onion
  • 3 coloured peppers
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 ribs celery
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cloves garlic, pressed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1,5 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 2 cans diced tomatoes
  • 1 carton tomato passata
  • 2 cans kidney beans
  • 1 can black beans
  • 2 can pinto beans (or cannellini beans)
  • 1 can corn
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander, plus some for garnishing
  • 2 teaspoons vinegar or lime juice, to taste

Chop the onion, bell pepper, carrot and celery.

Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat, then add the chopped vegetables and a 1/4 teaspoon salt.

Cook for about 10 minutes, then add the crushed garlic, chili powder, cumin and smoked paprika.

Stir and cook for about a minute, add the tomatoes, beans, corn, vegetable broth and the bay leaf.

Let all of it simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 30 minutes.

For the best texture, blend a small amound of the chili with an immersion blender or mash about 1,5 cups of it with a potato masher.

Add the chopped coriander, then season with vinegar and salt if needed.

Serve with:

Jacket Potatoes

Grilled Polenta
