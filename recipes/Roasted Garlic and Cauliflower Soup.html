Roasted Garlic and Cauliflower Soup

4 Servings

  • 1 head cauliflower
  • 3-4 heads garlic
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 shallots
  • 500ml vegetable broth
  • salt and pepper

Preheat the oven to 200°C. Peel off the outer layers of the garlic bulb, but leave to individual cloves intact and connected. Cut off the very top of the bulb, place it on a square of aluminum foil and coat each with ½ teaspoon of olive oil. Place in heated oven for 35 minutes.

Once complete, allow to cool slightly before removing from aluminum foil and squeezing out the garlic from each clove.

Meanwhile, chop the shallots and cut the cauliflower into florets. Pour remaining olive oil in a medium-sized saucepan and add the shallots. Saute until tender and beginning to brown on medium heat, about 6 minutes.

Add roasted garlic along with remaining ingredients to the saucepan. Cover and bring to a boil, reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the cauliflower is softened.

Blend the soup with an immersion blender until smooth. Adjust with salt and pepper and serve.
