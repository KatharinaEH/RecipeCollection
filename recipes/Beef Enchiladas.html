Beef Enchiladas

2 Servings

  • 4 corn tortillas
  • 400g/2 servings of Chili con Carne
  • black beans (or optionally 1 can black beans):
    • 100g dried black beans
    • 3 cloves garlic
    • 1 small onion
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • enchilada sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons margarine
    • 2 tablespoons flour
    • 1.5 tablespoons chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon nigella seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 400g can chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 avocado
  • 30g corn
  • 1 spring onion

Peel the onion and the garlic cloves, then put them, the dried black beans and 1/2 teaspoon of salt in about 600ml cold water on high heat. Once boiling, cook for 10 min on high heat. Turn the heat down to low and simmer for another 60 min. Refill water if necessary. After 60 min try the beans and if they are soft, put them on high heat, until they are dry.

Melt the margarine in a saucepan, then whisk in the flour. As soon as a paste has formed, mix in the spices and mix well. Leave on high heat for 20 seconds, then add some water to make it easier to mix with the tomatoes. Add the canned tomatoes as well as about 100ml more water and bring to a boil for 3-4 min.

Peel and slice the avocado. Slice the spring onion finely.

Spoon about 1/3 of the enchilada sauce in the bottom of a small baking tray, just big enough to fit the 4 tortillas. Then fill the tortillas with chili, black beans, avocado, corn, spring onion and a tablespoon of enchilada sauce each. Put them fold down into the pan and then pour the rest of the enchilada sauce over the top.

Bake for 20 min at about 180C, until slightly bubbling and browned on top.

Serve with:

Chicken instead of Chili con Carne

Guacomole, black bean spread, sour cream
