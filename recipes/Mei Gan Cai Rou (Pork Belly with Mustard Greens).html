Mei Gan Cai Rou (Pork Belly with Mustard Greens)

3 Servings

  • 1 generous handful of dried mustard greens
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 packet of pork belly (fatty)
  • 1 teaspoon dark soy sauce (for pork belly)
  • 3 tablespoons soft brown sugar (or light muscavado)
  • 1 large teaspoon minced ginger
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons shao-xing wine
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • 4 slices of ginger
  • 2 star anise

Wash the mustard greens thouroughly, optionally soak them in a minimum amount of water overnight.

Cut the strips of pork belly into 5 cm pieces. Sear the pork belly with a bit of oil in a pan over very high heat for a short time. After it is browned, pour on a teaspoon of dark soy sauce and a teaspoon of water. Keep heating it until it soaks up the soy sauce, then put to the side.

Meanwhile mix the dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, shao-xing wine and chicken stock together for later use.

Put the sugar in the pan, and cook over medium high heat in the remaining fat of the pan (or add some oil if necessary) until caramalized. Once its almost ready, add the minced ginger and stir fry for half a minute.

Add the mustard greens and stir fry for a minute. Add the soy sauce-chicken stock mixture, the sliced ginger and the star anise. Put the pork back in and keep cooking for a minimum of 2 hours over low heat. If dried out during cooking, add a bit of water.

Serve with:

Pak-Choi, Chinese Leave, Garlic Roasted Spinach, ...

Yue Xang Qie Zi (Aubergine in Fish-Fragrant Sauce)

Fan Qie Chao Dan (Stir Fried Tomato and Egg)
