Yue Xang Qie Zi (Aubergine in Fish-Fragrant Sauce)

2 Servings

  • 2 small-medium aubergines (about 600g)
  • salt (optional)
  • frying oil
  • sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons chili bean sauce (toban djan)
    • 60 ml chicken stock
    • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon Chiankiang vinegar (or balsamic vinegar)
    • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon yellow wine
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1.5 tabelspoons ginger, finely chopped
  • 1.5 tablespoons garlic, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon Sichuan peppers
  • 3 spring onions, cut into 4 cm sticks
  • 1 spring onion, finely chopped (for garnish)

Chop the aubergine in half lenghtwise, then half again. Then chop each strip into quarters. Optionally, sprinkle with salt and leave for 20 minutes. Drain before frying.

Heat 4 cm of oil in a saucepan to a high heat. Fry the aubergine chunks in small batches; they should turn brown quickly without soaking up too much oil and should keep their shape. Set aside in a warm bowl lined with kitchen paper.

Mix the chili bean sauce, chicken stock, cornstarch, Chiankian vinegar, light soy sauce, yellow wine, sugar, and sesame oil in a small bowl.

Heat the wok smoking hot, add 2 tablespoons of the frying oil. Add the garlic, Sichuan pepper and ginger. Fry for a few seconds, then add the spring onions. Before everything goes brown, add the aubergine and then the sauce.

Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.

Serve with:

sticky rice (60g per person)

Spicy Lotus Root

Garlic-fried Spinach or Pak Choi

Stir-fried French Beans
