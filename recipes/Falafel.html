Falafel

~30 falafel

  • 500g dried chickpeas
  • 1 onion
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • sesame seeds (optional for coating)
  • 1 litre oil for frying

Pour the chickpeas into a large bowl, cover with cold water and let soak overnight.

Drain the chickpeas and pour into food processor with all other ingredients. Pulse until rough, coarse meal forms, somewhere between the texture of couscous and a paste.

Heat a pan with the frying oil and form the mixture into falafels (either with a falafel spoon or use two soup spoons to form them. If wanted, roll them in sesame seeds to give them an even crunchier coating.

Brown the falafel for 2-3 minutes per side, starting with one to test if it stick together. If not, process a bit more or add some flour to the mixture.

Fry the rest of the falafel, then drain them on a paper towel and serve hot or cold.

Note: The mixture freezes well or keeps in the refrigerator for a good week.

Serve with:

Hummus

Pita
